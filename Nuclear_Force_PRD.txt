01 — PRODUCT ONE-LINER

A real-time, GPS-powered survival router that turns nuclear panic into a 3-second
tactical plan — identifying the nearest high-shielding shelter, drawing a visual
escape route, and visualizing blast/fallout threat radius on a single map.

================================================================================

02 — PROBLEM & SOLUTION

THE PROBLEM
Existing maps are built for normalcy. In a nuclear event, the internet floods with
misinformation and people don't know a safe building from a death trap. Every second
of searching = radiation exposure.

THE SOLUTION
Default-to-Shelter logic: the moment you open the app, it already knows the nearest
high-shielding structure, the walking time, and the depth you need to reach. No
searching required.

================================================================================

03 — CORE FEATURES (MVP SCOPE)

The app is organized into 3 tabs: Find Shelter | Threat Analysis | Evacuate

TAB 1 — FIND SHELTER

  - Survival Directive Card [P0] — Hero card on load: nearest high-shielding location
    (subway, reinforced garage, hospital basement), walking time, required depth.
    Auto-triggers on GPS lock.

  - Visual Escape Route [P0] — Blue lifeline polyline from user GPS to nearest shelter
    on Leaflet map. 4 shelter types: Subway, Hospital, Parking Garage, Basement.
    Tappable markers with shielding factor label.

TAB 2 — THREAT ANALYSIS

  - Blast Radius + Fallout Plume [P0] — Client-side geometric analysis: concentric
    danger-zone circles (fireball / heavy blast / light blast / fallout), and a
    directional fallout cone driven by prevailing wind vector. No server calls needed.

TAB 3 — EVACUATE

  - Vehicle Evacuation Route [P1] — For users outside the immediate blast zone: a
    drive-out route drawn perpendicular to the fallout plume direction, routing away
    from downwind corridors. Straight vector line as hackathon fallback.
