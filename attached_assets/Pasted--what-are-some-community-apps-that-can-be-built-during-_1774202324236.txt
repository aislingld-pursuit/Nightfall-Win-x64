⭕ How Blast Rings Actually Work

The math is just circles with known radii. Nuclear blast data is completely public — Alex Wellerstein's NukeMap (nuclearsecrecy.com) is the academic gold standard and all the data is published.
For a 1 megaton warhead (standard Cold War city-buster), the rings are:
RingRadiusColorWhat HappensFireball1.1 km🔴 Dark RedEverything vaporizedHeavy Blast4.7 km🟠 OrangeConcrete buildings destroyedModerate Blast7.6 km🟡 YellowWidespread structural damageThermal Radiation11 km🟣 PurpleThird degree burns, firesLight Blast17 km🔵 BlueWindows shattered, some injuries
You let the user pick the weapon yield — small dirty bomb, Hiroshima-size, or full megaton. The radii just scale with yield using published formulas.
